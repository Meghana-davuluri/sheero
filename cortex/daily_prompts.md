# Daily Prompts — Growth & Productivity

Used by the morning check-in skill. After logging the morning journal, the skill identifies the dominant theme and pulls one prompt from that section.

If no theme matches clearly, use the **General** section.

Rotate — don't repeat the same prompt within 7 days. Pick a prompt your hasn't seen recently when possible.

---

## Self-Criticism

When the journal sounds like "I should have," "I'm not doing enough," or blame/shame at yourself.

- What's *one* concrete action today that turns self-criticism into evidence it was wrong?
- If a friend wrote this same journal entry, what would you tell them to do next?
- The past hour is sunk. What's the smallest 10-minute win available right now?
- Name the behavior you're criticizing. Now design a system so it's easier to do the opposite tomorrow.
- Would this mistake matter in 6 months? If not, what *would* matter that you can work on instead?

---

## Inaction

When the journal shows being stuck, avoidant, overwhelmed, or unable to start.

- What's the 5-minute version of the task that feels too big?
- Which task on your list, if finished today, would make the other tasks feel easier?
- Open the thing. Just open it. What's the next move once it's open?
- What are you actually avoiding — the work, or a decision about the work?
- Lower the bar: what would a "bad first draft" look like? Ship that instead.

---

## Acceptance

When the journal shows resignation, coming-to-terms, settling.

- What are you accepting — the situation, or giving up on changing it?
- What's the single variable in this situation you *can* still move?
- If this is the new baseline, what's the best possible move from here?
- Write down the thing you're accepting. Now write one thing you refuse to accept.
- Acceptance is fuel, not rest — what will you do with the clarity it gives you today?

---

## Gratitude

When the journal is grounded, appreciative, or thankful.

- Who or what made this week possible? Compound it — how do you give more of that energy?
- What worked this week that you should stop calling "lucky" and start calling "a pattern"?
- Gratitude without action fades. What's the follow-through today?
- Who needs to hear this from you — and do they know?
- If today's momentum continued for 30 days, what would change?

---

## Frustration

When the journal shows anger, blocked-ness, friction, or resentment.

- What is this frustration pointing to that you've been ignoring?
- What's ONE decision you can make today that reduces this friction by tomorrow?
- Is the frustration with a person, a system, or yourself? Each has a different fix.
- What would the version of you from 5 years ago be thrilled you're even frustrated about?
- If you had to solve this frustration in the next 60 minutes, what would you try first?

---

## Clarity

When the journal shows focus, resolve, or a clear direction.

- You have a rare window. What's the single highest-leverage task to do while clarity lasts?
- What would finishing this week make possible next week?
- Name the ONE thing that, if done today, makes everything else easier or irrelevant.
- Cut the list in half. Now cut it in half again. What's left?
- Clarity is a resource — don't waste it on small things. What's the big swing today?

---

## General (no clear theme)

- What's the most important thing on your list today — not the most urgent, the most important?
- If you could only finish ONE task today and the rest didn't exist, which would it be?
- What's a small improvement to your setup or process that would save you time all week?
- Who's waiting on you? Close that loop first.
- What would you tell yourself to do today if you wanted to make next week easier?
